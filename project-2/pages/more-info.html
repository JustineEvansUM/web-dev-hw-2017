In 1947, Kroger opened its first egg-processing plant in Wabash, Indiana, in order to further ensure egg quality. Decisions about products and methods of selling were to come from the “votes” shoppers left at the cash register. This was a time of rapid growth for supermarkets. Between 1948 and 1963, the number of supermarkets in the country nearly tripled. Kroger was already testing the specialty shops that would later be integral to its "superstores."
